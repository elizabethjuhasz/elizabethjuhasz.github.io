About

Born and raised in Miami, Florida. One of the great things about growing up in South Florida is that I learned early on about importance of environmental protection. I recieved a Bachelor's degree from Loyola University New Orleans where I double-majored in philosophy and psychology. After college, I went to an international school in Budapest, and studied Hungarian. I traveled throughout the Balkans, and began asking complex questions. I knew I had to go back to school. I came back to Miami, and worked temporarily at a bookstore. I loved working in sales, but I got in trouble for using Wikipedia during work hours. Once again, I was reminded that I had to go to graduate school, except at the time, it became "now or never."
I always had a strong interest in international relations, and went to the University of New Orleans originally as a Master's student with a first concentration in international relations and a second concentration in comparative politics. I then transferred into the PhD program. My dissertation looks at how politics affects refugee status granting to asylum-seekers. I orginally delve into the subject around 2013, when I noticed that it was a much ignored topic in international relations at the time. I mostly research migration, but my interests have expanded since then. Being in the state of Louisiana for some time, and working in Higher Ed, I became very passionate about Higher Ed policy. I'm a strong advocate for open science, academic freedom, and net neutrality. I currently work as a part-time analyst at Miami Dade College, and I love my job. I am interested in climate-induced migration, and how natural resources affects asylum granting. However, I take a strong interest in merging topics like Earth Science, Geography, and other environmental sciences with political science. My interests are wide, and I'm normally open to a wide range of topics. I enjoy taking pictures and traveling thanks to my parents. I don't travel to vacation, but rather, to learn about myself, others, and the world.
